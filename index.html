Sebastian Fernandez-Mulligan

I am pursuing a PhD in the History of Science and Medicine at Yale University. My research relates to the implementation of mathematical tools in the 19th/20th centuries. I am particularly interested in the (re-)use of these tools outside their original disciplines and the residue that is left on the math as it crosses epistemic and social boundaries in science.

Before arriving at Yale, I worked as a researcher in condensed matter physics. Back then, I studied non-equilibrium electronic states, with the goal of understanding the interplay between magnetism and topology in candidate materials for high-efficiency electronics.
