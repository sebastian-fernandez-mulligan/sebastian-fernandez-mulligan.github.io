Historical Research

My historical research studies the use of entropy in disciplines other than physics throughout the twentieth century. I touch on fields such as cybernetics, economics, symbolic logic, applied mathematics, sculpture, architecture, and cultural theory.

Scientific Research

My scientific work used high-spatial-resolution and time-resolved Angle-Resolved Photoemission spectroscopy to understand the relationship between magnetism, topology and the electronic properties for the MnBi2nTe3n+1 family of materials.
