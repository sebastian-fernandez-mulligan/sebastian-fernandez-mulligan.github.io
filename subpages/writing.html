Historical Research

My historical research studies the use of entropy in social theory during the twentieth century. I have also written about the history of astronomy.

Scientific Research

My scientific work used high-spatial-resolution and time-resolved Angle-Resolved Photoemission spectroscopy to understand the relationship between magnetism, topology and the electronic properties for the MnBi2nTe3n+1 family of materials.

  1. W. Lee*, S. Fernandez-Mulligan*, H. Tan, C. Yan, Y. Guan, S. H. Lee, R. Mei, C.-X. Liu, B. Yan, Z. Mao, and S.-L. Yang. "Layer-by-Layer Disentanglement of Bloch States." Nature Physics (2023) [Joint first author] paper
  2. C. Yan, Y. Zhu, L. Miao, S. Fernandez-Mulligan, E. Green, R. Mei, B. Yan, N. Alem, C.-X. Liu, Z. Mao, S.-L. Yang. "Delicate Ferromagnetism in MnBi6Te10." Nano Letters (2022) [Front cover!] paper
  3. C. Yan, E. Green, R. Fukumori, N. Protic, S. Fernandez-Mulligan, R. Raja, R. Erdakos, S.-L. Yang. "An Integrated Quantum Material Testbed with Multi-Resolution Photoemission Spectroscopy." Review of Scientific Instruments (2021) paper
  4. C. Yan, S. Fernandez-Mulligan, R. Mei, S. H. Lee, N. Protic, R. Fukumori, B. Yan, C.-X. Liu, Z. Mao, S.-L. Yang. "Origins of electronic bands in the antiferromagnetic topological insulator MnBi2Te4." Physical Review B (2021) paper
